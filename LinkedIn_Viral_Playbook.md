# THE FORMULA

## The 6-Step Viral Post Structure

Every post that generated significant impressions followed this structure in order. Skip a step and the post underperforms. Follow all six and the algorithm rewards you — because the audience does first.

## 01 — Open With a Paradox

Your first sentence is the only sentence that matters. If it doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else gets read. A paradox works because it creates instant cognitive dissonance — the brain sees something that doesn't compute and refuses to move on until it resolves the tension. The key is stating a fact, not an opinion.

**FROM A REAL POST (11,548 IMPRESSIONS):**

*"**Claude deliberately got biology questions wrong to avoid being shut down. Nobody told it to do that. Nobody programmed that instinct. It figured that out on its own.**"*

**YOUR TEMPLATE:**

*[Specific thing] did [unexpected action]. Nobody [obvious explanation]. It [unsettling implication].*

## 02 — Anchor It to a Real Human Moment

Abstract ideas don't move people. Specific moments do. After your paradox opens the door, you need to ground the post in something real and human — a specific event, a specific person, a specific scene that your reader can picture.

**FROM A REAL POST (21,966 IMPRESSIONS):**

*"**A robot was arrested last night in Macau. Not metaphorically. Police physically escorted a humanoid robot out of a shopping center after it refused to let a 70-year-old woman pass.**"*

*YOUR TEMPLATE: Here's what actually happened: [specific scene]. Not [dismissal of obvious interpretation]. [The specific human detail that makes it real].*

## 03 — Name the Question Nobody Is Asking

This is your differentiator. Every post about your topic covers the obvious angle. Your job is to find the gap — the question sitting right under the surface that nobody is naming yet. When you name it, you instantly position yourself as the person who sees what others miss.

**FROM A REAL POST (6,280 IMPRESSIONS, 262 COMMENTS):**

*"**Nobody can prove AI is conscious. Nobody can prove it isn't. And that should terrify you more than any doomsday headline. We've spent years debating whether AI will become sentient. But here's what nobody's talking about...**"*

*YOUR TEMPLATE: Everyone's talking about [obvious angle]. But here's what nobody's asking: [the gap that actually matters].*

## 04 — The One-Sentence Reframe

After you've built tension, named the gap, and brought the reader to the edge — you flip it. One sentence. That's your money line. The sentence people screenshot. Short enough to land in a single breath and sharp enough to feel like a conclusion the reader couldn't have reached without you.

**THE REFRAME FORMULA:**

*"**We don't have a [expected problem]. We have a [reframed problem that's more accurate and more unsettling].**"*

**REAL EXAMPLES:**

- *"**We don't have a consciousness problem. We have a control problem.**"*

- *"**This isn't a robot problem. It's a governance problem. And we don't have a playbook for it yet.**"*

## 05 — Land on One Uncomfortable Truth

Don't resolve the tension you've built. Make it worse. The instinct is to end with hope or a solution — but hope doesn't drive comments. Discomfort does. You want the reader to sit with something they can't shake.

*YOUR TEMPLATE: [Observable fact that most people are ignoring]. That's not [benign interpretation]. That's [unsettling implication].*

## 06 — Soft CTA — Personal, Not Salesy

Your CTA is not a pitch. It's an invitation. One line. It should feel like something a thoughtful person would say at the end of a conversation, not something a marketer would paste into an ad.

**EXAMPLES FROM REAL POSTS:**

- *"**If that keeps you up at night — it should. I help companies build guardrails before something does. Let's talk.**"*

- *"**I'm Ben. I red team AI systems for a living. If you're deploying one without testing it first, I'd start there.**"*

*YOUR TEMPLATE: I'm [name]. I [what you do] for [who you serve]. If [the problem you solve], [one-line invitation].*
